About me

I am currently a Master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Georgia Institute of Technology (Georgia Tech), where I am conducting research at the Uncommon Sense Lab with Prof. Alexander T. Adams. My current work focuses on developing ubiquitous sensing and infant-specific respiratory monitoring systems for SIDS prevention, exploring novel, non-invasive sensing modalities to advance equitable health technologies.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from New York University (NYU), where I graduated with University Honors (Founders Day Award) and a top ECE major GPA. At NYU, I joined the AI4CE Lab led by Prof. Chen Feng, where I worked as a research assistant and later team lead. My work spanned Embodied AI, mobile 3D printing, and soft robotic acoustic sensing, with a focus on integrating embedded systems, sensors, and ROS-based platforms into real-world robotic agents.

In parallel, I conducted interdisciplinary research at NYU Langone Health under Prof. Shuo Chen, developing ubiquitous embedded experimental systems for neuroscience, enabling automated behavioral and memory data collection in rodents.

Beyond academia, I gained industrial experience as an Embedded and IoT Design Engineer intern at Nanjing Qinheng Microelectronics Co., contributing to RISC-V MCU development boards and PCB layouts for USB-to-serial products.

My research interests lie at the intersection of ubiquitous computing, embedded intelligence, and human-centered systems. I aim to design interpretable, resource-efficient robotic and sensing platforms that bridge advanced AI with real-world health and robotics applications.

I am actively seeking a Ph.D. position for Fall 2026.
