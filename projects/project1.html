Vision-Based Infant Respiratory Monitoring and SIDS Detection

A step forward from previous project, Designed a fully non-invasive, vision-based infant monitoring system. Using AprilTags in daylight and reflective markers under infrared (IR) illumination at night, this enables robust breathing and posture detection without any physical sensors on the infant.

Developing novel sensing and feedback mechanisms by leveraging computer vision algorithms to extract breathing waveforms, track posture/orientation, and generate alerts for apnea, unsafe posture, or off-center sleeping.

Equity-drivened to ensure affordability, compatibility with existing baby cameras or smartphones, and accessibility for families across diverse socioeconomic backgrounds.
