Vision-Based Infant Respiratory Monitoring and SIDS Detection

May 2025 - Now

Junyang Tang

A step forward from my previous project, I designed a fully non-invasive, vision-based infant monitoring system. Using AprilTags in daylight and reflective markers under infrared (IR) illumination at night, this enables robust breathing and posture detection without any physical sensors on the infant.

I developed novel sensing and feedback mechanisms by leveraging computer vision algorithms to extract breathing waveforms, track posture/orientation, and generate alerts for apnea, unsafe posture, or off-center sleeping.

This system was equity-driven, designed for affordability and compatibility with existing baby monitors or smartphones, ensuring accessibility for families across diverse socioeconomic backgrounds.
