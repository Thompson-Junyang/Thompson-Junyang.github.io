Force Sensing Sudden Infant Death Syndrome Detection IOT System

January 2025 - May 2025

Junyang Tang

At Georgia Tech, under the supervision of Dr. Diego Fratta, I designed and built a ubiquitous, low-power IoT-based infant monitoring system that integrates seamlessly with everyday devices such as smartphones, PCs, and baby monitors, enabling real-time access to health and safety data. The system features a flexible force sensor combined with digital signal processing (DSP) algorithms to detect shallow or absent breathing, triggering multimodal alerts—including an LED ring, audio alarms, and a remote caregiver dashboard. This novel sensing and feedback framework offers a non-invasive approach to Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS) detection by monitoring respiration, temperature, and ambient light to ensure comprehensive infant safety.
