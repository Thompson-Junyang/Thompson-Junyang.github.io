Mobile 3D Printing & Large scale 3D Clay Printing

May 2022 - July 2024

Junyang Tang

As a Research Assistant at New York University's AI4CE Lab (Advisor: Prof. Chen Feng), I contributed to the development of a mobile 3D printing system using learning-based visual servoing, which was later adapted for large-scale clay printing. The system was capable of autonomously following predesigned print trails guided by a projection-based path planner. I designed and assembled a custom robotic platform integrating a Jetson single board computer, microcontrollers, RGB-D cameras, and a LiDAR sensor. My responsibilities included setting up Ubuntu Linux and the Robot Operating System (ROS) on the Jetson, programming ROS nodes, and configuring sensor drivers using I2C/SPI communication protocols. I also debugged LiDAR sensor precision and accuracy, and designed custom PCBs for circuit-level integration, ensuring reliable performance in real-world printing tasks.
