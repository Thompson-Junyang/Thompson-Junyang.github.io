Embedded Experiment Setups to Examine Mice Neurological State

May 2022 - May 2023

Junyang Tang

As a Research Assistant at NYU Langone Health (Wisniewski and Masurkar Labs, Advisor: Prof. Shuo Chen), I developed an embedded-controlled maze system to automatically record rats' behavioral and memory data for neuroscience research. I designed and assembled the maze using an AVR RISC-based microcontroller, integrating IR sensors, servo motors, and valves for dynamic, automated experimentation. I also designed the schematic and PCB layout for a custom 12-volt motor/valve driver board utilizing Darlington transistors, and implemented remote control functionality alongside low-level firmware to manage I/O and sensor logic. The system was successfully deployed and tested by medical students at NYU School of Medicine, significantly improving experimental efficiency and reducing operational costs. The device's success was recognized in the conference presentation “Hypothalamic Theta Modulation Improves Memory” at Neuroscience 2023 (Tracking No. 2023-S-14818-SFN).
